## Interdisciplinary Context: Anti-Icarus

### Cybernetics & Systems Theory
The observer problem in second-order cybernetics (H. von Foerster): the observer is inseparable from the system, and their state influences it. Anti-Icarus formalizes the observer's state through viability metrics (VVM) and introduces protocols for managing this state, turning the «human factor» from a blind spot into a manageable system parameter.

### Philosophy
The myth of Icarus as a warning against hubris and detachment from reality. Anti-Icarus reframes the myth: Icarus's fall is not a consequence of hubris, but an architectural defect — the absence of an honest critic and a grounding circuit. The principle proposes an ethics of self-care (M. Foucault) as an integral part of design.

### Psychology
Research on burnout, cognitive isolation, and the «expert trap». Anti-Icarus offers preventive protocols: the «Heresy Inoculation» — controlled cognitive dissonance; the «Grounding Circuit» — mindfulness practices and quick wins; the «Ethical Switch» — permission to rest without guilt.
